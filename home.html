    Domain

    Research Question

    Can Virtual Reality simulations effectively enhance military navigation training by providing immersive experiences for mastering intersection, resection, artillery firing, and silver compass reading, ultimately improving soldiers' readiness and proficiency in real-world scenarios?

    Research Gap

    "Foundation Military Training through Digitalization via Mixed Reality’s Opportunities and Challenges," addresses several critical gaps in traditional military training methods. These include the lack of comprehensive integration of key training components such as Intersection, Resection, Silver Compass reading, and Artillery Firing into a unified VR-based system. Traditional training methods often fail to provide the necessary realism and immersive experiences, are resource-intensive, and do not cater to the diverse learning needs of individual soldiers. Additionally, there is limited research on the long-term retention and transferability of skills acquired through VR training and the implementation of effective feedback and continuous improvement mechanisms.

    Literature Review

    The ability to navigate is critical for military operations since it allows soldiers to over diverse terrain and ensures that mission goals are accomplished effectively and precisely. Like many countries, Sri Lanka has traditionally relied largely on classroom instruction, field exercises, and simulations as part of its conventional navigation training methods. Although these approaches have been useful in the past, they have drawbacks that make it difficult for them to effectively prepare soldiers for the changing and unpredictable nature of modern combat.

    Research Objectives

    Evaluate Virtual Reality (VR) effectiveness in teaching military navigation skills. Compare VR training with traditional methods to assess learning outcomes. Recommend improvements and practical applications of VR for military navigation training.navigation skills

    Methodology

    "Foundation Military Training through Digitalization via Mixed Reality’s Opportunities and Challenges," involves several key phases: requirement analysis, system design and development, implementation, data collection, evaluation, and continuous improvement. Initially, a thorough literature review and stakeholder consultation will be conducted to gather insights and requirements. Subsequently, immersive VR environments and realistic training scenarios for Intersection, Resection, Silver Compass reading, and Artillery Firing will be developed. After building and pilot testing the prototype, we will collect quantitative performance metrics and qualitative usability feedback from soldiers. The data will be analyzed to evaluate the effectiveness and usability of the VR system compared to traditional training methods, with continuous refinements based on feedback. Ethical considerations such as informed consent and confidentiality will be strictly maintained throughout the study. This methodology aims to rigorously assess and optimize the potential of VR technology to enhance military training and operational effectiveness.
